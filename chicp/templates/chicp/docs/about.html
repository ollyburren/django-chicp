About

CHiCP is a web application for visually integrating human population genetic data with promoter capture Hi-C data. It was primarily developed to support the analysis described in Javierre et al (2016) Cell. We incorporate autoimmune focused population genetic data (GWAS and ImmunoChip) from ImmunoBase as these are the focus of our research. However, please contact us if you are interested in hosting your data (GWAS or contact maps), either on this instance, or as part of your own installation.

CHiCP (https://www.chicp.org) is currently maintained and developed by Olly Burren in the Wallace Group, within the Department of Medicine at the University of Cambridge. Hosting and continued development is supported by the Wellcome Trust.

How To Cite

If you use CHiCP in your work please cite:   Schofield EC, Carver T, Achuthan P, Freire-Pritchett P, Spivakov M, Todd JA, Burren OS. CHiCP: a web-based tool for the integrative and interactive visualization of promoter capture Hi-C datasets. Bioinformatics. (2016) 15:32(16):2511-3 url along with the date accessed.

How to Run Your Own CHiCP

We have developed an experimental prototype Docker repository which is available on GitHub, or you can download the latest build from here. Please contact us (see top navigation bar) for more information if you get stuck.
